Hook Sizes

Almost any yarn, except perhaps home-made yarn, will have a label displaying the hook size marked in millimeters (mm), and sometimes by another size (marked below). However, if you are unsure, that's okay! It doesn't really matter what hook you use as long as you're comfortable. The point of having the correct hook is the best balance between being able to "grab" the yarn (a sign of using a too-small hook) and having to force your hook through each stitch or having very loose stitches (signs of using one too big). Find one that is comfortable for you!

Yarn Weight

Again, almost all yarn will have a label showing the weight of the yarn as a number between 0 and 7. Depending on how thin you want your mittens to be, I recommend using size 4 to 7. I used a size 4 (worsted), but doubled the yarn for a tighter, thicker result. Remember, the bigger your yarn is, the less stitches you'll need. However, it will also be less precise and have larger "steps" on increases and decreases, rather than a rounded look.
